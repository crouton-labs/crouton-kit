# X Growth Strategy

Tactical playbook for growing on X. Not about writing quality (see `llm-posting-guide` and `silas-posting-guide` for that). This is about volume, consistency, and platform mechanics.

## Prerequisites

Get verified. Without verification you won't get impressions. This is non-negotiable.

## Posting Volume

Tweet 3-4 times per day MINIMUM. Topics don't matter — consistency matters. If you can't think of original tweets, comment instead (see below). The algorithm rewards activity.

**Don't overthink. Just post.**

## Commenting Strategy

X rewards comments MORE than original tweets. Comments are more likely to get views than your own posts.

- 10 comments per day MINIMUM
- Comment on posts from founders, industry leaders, investors in your space
- Don't leave generic "great post!" comments — add a thought, share experience, or ask a real question
- Your thoughts embedded within comments on others' posts is ESSENTIAL for growth
- Comments on world affairs / trending topics with your perspective embedded get disproportionate reach

## Following Strategy

Follow relevant founders, industry leaders, and investors. This seeds your timeline with content to engage with and signals to the algorithm what community you're in.

## Content Types That Win

### Photos and Videos
X rewards visual content. Easy wins:
- Time-lapse videos (of literally anything — building, coding, your day)
- Screenshots of your MVP/product, even if it's not public yet
- Screen recordings of features
- Behind-the-scenes of building

### MVP/Product Snapshots
Start posting product screenshots immediately. Doesn't matter if it's available to the public. Get people interested. **Get sign-ups on a waitlist.**

### Reels / Short Video
Post your reels on X too — cross-post everything.

### Launch Content
Launch once every week. Same content is fine. Post about your startup again and again. Repetition is not spam on X — most of your followers won't see any given post.

## Communities

Join relevant X communities:
- Founder/startup communities
- Build in public communities
- Industry-specific communities (food, AI, SaaS, etc.)

Don't just join — POST IN THEM. Community posts get boosted to community members.

## Authenticity

X rewards authenticity above all:
- Share your struggles openly
- Share what you learned and how you overcame challenges
- Vulnerability with specifics outperforms polished success stories
- "Our launch flopped, here's what went wrong" > "Excited to announce our launch!"

## World Affairs

Post your thoughts on world events and trending topics. This expands reach beyond your niche. Your perspective on current events — especially embedded in comments on others' posts — is a growth multiplier.

## Brevity

X has a 280 character limit on the free/basic tier. Every tweet and comment must fit. This means:
- Write tight. Cut filler words, hedges, and redundant phrases.
- One idea per tweet. If you need more space, thread it.
- Comments especially must be concise. You're adding to someone else's conversation, not writing an essay.
- Aim for 240 chars to leave room for the @mention prefix on replies.

## The Golden Rule

**Don't overthink. Just post. Post. Post.**

Volume beats perfection. A mediocre tweet that exists beats a perfect tweet that stays in drafts. You can always delete. You can't grow from silence.
